About This Project

Endometrium Cancer Detection Using CNN

Endometrial cancer, also known as uterine cancer, is a prevalent malignancy affecting the inner lining of the uterus ‚Äî the endometrium. It ranks as one of the most common gynecological cancers globally, with its incidence steadily increasing over the years. While it primarily affects postmenopausal women, cases among premenopausal women are also observed, making it a significant health concern across various age groups.


The endometrium plays a crucial role in the reproductive cycle, undergoing cyclic changes in response to hormonal fluctuations. However, when these regulatory mechanisms go awry, abnormal cell growth can occur, leading to the development of endometrial cancer. This system uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) trained on histopathological images to classify tissue samples into four categories: Adenocarcinoma, Hyperplasia, Polyp, and Normal Endometrium.
